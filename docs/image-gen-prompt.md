# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished West African kamele ngoni bowl-lute presented as a real physical object, photographed at a 3/4 product-photography angle with an approximately 50mm lens. The instrument's defining silhouette: a deep rounded segmented wooden bowl with a creamy off-white goatskin head stretched taut across its open face and tacked around the rim, with a single long slender round hardwood neck rising from the head-rim end of the bowl up toward the upper-left of the frame. The bowl is built from stacked, glued rings of Black Walnut — warm chocolate-brown wood whose vertical segment glue lines and ring-stack banding are clearly visible across the curved exterior as an intentional design feature, not a flaw, with a soft hand-rubbed tung-oil sheen. Ten plain nylon monofilament strings of graduated thickness run from a low broad-footed Hard Maple bridge that rests directly on the goatskin (not glued, held by string tension) in a fan toward the neck, all on one side of the neck, anchored at the tail and rising along the maple neck to small leather konso tuning rings wrapped around the neck near the headstock. Realistic scale cues: the bowl reads roughly the size of a large mixing bowl, the neck about as long as a forearm-and-a-half. Clean neutral light-grey studio background, soft directional studio light raking gently across the goatskin and walnut to reveal grain and segment lines, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same Kamele 10-string ngoni staged in a warm woodworking shop near a window, resting upright with the goatskin head facing the viewer and the maple neck leaning against a workbench scattered with wood shavings and a spokeshave. Natural window light pours in from the side, catching the cream goatskin and the chocolate-brown walnut ring-stack of the bowl, with soft environmental storytelling — a turned bowl blank or band clamp faintly visible in the background. Shallow depth of field keeps the strings, bridge, and konso rings crisp while the shop softens into bokeh. Concept visualization only, photorealistic, warm natural color, no text.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the ngoni's signature element: the low broad-footed Hard Maple bridge resting directly on the stretched goatskin head, its gently crowned top notched to seat ten graduated nylon strings that bear down over it at a shallow break angle. Raking side light reveals the fine pores and grain of the maple, the slightly translucent fibrous texture of the dried goatskin with faint tack marks around the distant rim, and the subtle sheen of the nylon strings under tension. The walnut bowl rim and visible segment glue lines sit softly out of focus behind. High detail, photorealistic, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms (bridge stays seated by string tension, strings stay on one side of the neck); calabash gourds shown only as wood — depict the segmented walnut bowl, not a natural gourd; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
