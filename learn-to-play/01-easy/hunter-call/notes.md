# Hunter's Call

**Provenance:** Adapted from a traditional Mande hunter's praise pattern.
These short melodic call figures are part of the donkili (praise song) tradition
and are in the public domain as oral folk heritage.

**Range:** D3–F♯5. Uses the full 5-string D pentatonic range.

**Performance context:** Hunter's praise pieces (donkili) are played by griots
to celebrate successful hunters. The short call-and-response phrases (4 bars,
rest, 4 bars) mimic the hunter signalling to the village.

**Practice tips:**
- Play the first bar (D4 ^F2 A2) as a thumb-index-index pattern: thumb hits D,
  index hits F♯, index hits A.
- The long d8 (D5, 4 beats) in bar 4 is your chance to let the melody string
  ring while your thumb adds the D3 drone beneath.
