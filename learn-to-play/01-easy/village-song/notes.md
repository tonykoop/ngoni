# Village Song

**Provenance:** Original melody in traditional Mande style. Draws on the
kumbengo (ostinato) patterns common in West African village music. MIT licensed.

**Range:** D3–F♯5.

**Character:** A calm, cyclical tune meant to loop 4–8 times without variation
before adding ornamentation.

**Practice tips:**
- Play this as a continuous loop — don't stop at the bar line, just keep going.
  After 3 repetitions you'll start to feel the groove rather than read the notes.
- Add a thumb drone on D3 on beat 1 of every other bar (bars 1, 3, 5, 7).
- Once the loop is comfortable, try adding a slide (`sl.`) into the first F♯
  of each phrase from D — that small embellishment is the whole vocabulary of
  the intermediate ngoni style.
