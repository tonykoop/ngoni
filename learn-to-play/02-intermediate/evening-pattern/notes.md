# Evening Pattern

**Provenance:** Original composition in Mande style. MIT licensed.

**Range:** D3–F♯5. 12/8 compound time.

**Why intermediate:**
- 12/8 feel requires internalising a triplet subdivision while keeping steady
  thumb-bass pulse.
- The long tones (D9 = 3 beats) demand sustained tone from a fretless pluck —
  the note must be pure and not decay too fast.

**Character:** This pattern evokes the quieter ngoni playing heard at the
end of the day — less driving, more meditative than the dance pieces.

**Practice tips:**
- Count "one-trip-let, two-trip-let" for each dotted-quarter beat.
- The thumb plays the D on beat 1 of each 3-beat group; the fingers play
  the melody notes on beats 2 and 3.
- Keep the body of the instrument resting loosely on your thigh — tension in
  the grip position kills the sustain on the long tones.
