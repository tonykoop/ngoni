# Ngoni — Jig Decision Guide

**Instrument:** Ngoni (Kamele N'goni)  
**Date:** 2026-05-08

For each significant fabrication step, this document answers: *Do I need a custom jig, a standard fixture, or neither?*

---

## Jig Decisions by Step

### 1. Segment Miter Cuts (18°)

**Decision:** Standard miter sled with stop block — **no custom jig needed**  
**Rationale:** An 18° miter is a standard table-saw setup. Set once and batch-cut all segments for all rings.  
**Setup:** Miter sled set to 18°; stop block set to segment length per ring. Use featherboard on infeed side.  
**Verify:** Cut a test ring with 10 scrap segments; dry-fit; check flatness before committing to walnut stock.

---

### 2. Ring Glue-Up (10-segment)

**Decision:** Band clamp — **no custom jig needed**  
**Rationale:** A 10–14 in band clamp provides even pressure around the ring. The critical step is having all segments pre-set on a flat surface before tightening.  
**Alternative:** If band clamp is unavailable, use rubber banding or hose clamps with a smooth-faced caul.  
**Note:** Glue one ring at a time; do not attempt to glue multiple rings simultaneously without a dedicated stacking fixture.

---

### 3. Ring Stack and Glue (11 rings)

**Decision:** Pipe clamps through the bowl axis — **simple fixture, no custom jig**  
**Rationale:** Two pipe clamps spanning top-to-bottom through the open bowl center provide enough clamping pressure for the stack glue-up.  
**Caul:** Use flat MDF cauls on both ends (base and head ring) to distribute pressure.  
**Procedure:** Stack all rings with glue; clamp on flat reference surface; check for twist before setting.

---

### 4. Lathe Mounting — Rough Bowl Blank

**Decision:** Between-centers mounting with a live center — **standard lathe setup**  
**Rationale:** The bowl blank is cylindrical enough to center between a drive spur and a live tail center.  
**Caution:** Initial blank is heavy and out-of-balance. Start at < 500 RPM; bring speed up gradually only after blank is trued.  
**Alternative:** Large 4-jaw chuck if available (preferred for hollow turning — gives access to interior without tail center obstruction).

---

### 5. Neck-Entry Hole (1.25 in Ø through head-rim ring)

**Decision:** Drill press with a V-block cradle — **simple fixture needed**  
**Rationale:** The finished bowl is a curved object; it needs support in a V-block or cradle to hold it flat and perpendicular on the drill press table.  
**Jig spec:** A simple V-block from scrap wood holds the bowl rim face perpendicular to the drill bit axis. The V-block can be clamped or screwed to the drill press table.  
**Alternative:** Drill press with a riser block and hand-hold if V-block is unavailable — but perpendicularity is critical here.

---

### 6. Bridge Notch Layout

**Decision:** Layout template — **simple layout jig (paper or MDF)**  
**Rationale:** 10 notches at exactly 8 mm spacing (center-to-center) are most consistently laid out with a printed or MDF spacing template.  
**Template spec:** Print or cut a 72 mm (total spread) × 1 mm bridge template; align to bridge centerline; mark notch positions with awl before carving.  
**Tool:** Triangular file or 1 mm round file for notch cutting; CNC mill if using the CNC.

---

### 7. Neck Radius / Profile Shaping

**Decision:** No jig — **spokeshave + rasp by hand**  
**Rationale:** Ngoni neck is a simple round or D-profile; no complex taper like a guitar neck. A spokeshave produces consistent results on straight-grain maple without a router table or CNC fixture.  
**Optional:** Use a lathe if the neck is perfectly straight and the lathe has sufficient length (30 in between centers).  
**Note:** The bowl-entry end should be slightly oversized and sneak-fit into the bowl hole by hand.

---

### 8. Skin Stretching

**Decision:** Tacking tack pattern only — **no jig needed**  
**Rationale:** Traditional skin stretching is done by hand and eye. The key is a consistent star-pattern tacking sequence (N–S–E–W then diagonals) not a mechanical fixture.  
**Tension gauge:** Optional — a drum skin tension gauge (e.g., Ludwig Gauge-O-Matic) can verify cross-skin consistency. Not required for first prototype.

## Jig Summary Table

| Step | Jig Needed? | Type | Build or Buy | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Segment miter cuts | No | Standard miter sled | Already in shop | High |
| Ring glue-up | No | Band clamp | Buy if not in shop | High |
| Ring stack glue-up | Minimal | Pipe clamps + MDF cauls | Already in shop | High |
| Lathe mounting | No | Standard centers or 4-jaw | Already in shop | High |
| Neck-entry hole | Yes | V-block cradle | Build from scrap (30 min) | High |
| Bridge notch layout | Yes | Paper / MDF template | Make (5 min) | Medium |
| Neck shaping | No | Spokeshave + rasp | Already in shop | High |
| Skin stretching | No | Tacking pattern | No fixture needed | Medium |
